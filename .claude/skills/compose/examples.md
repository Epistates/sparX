# High-Performing Post Examples — Calibration Reference

These examples demonstrate the quality bar. Study the patterns, not the specific content.

## Insight Post (Tech)

**What makes it work**: Specific number, unexpected finding, invites debate

```
We switched from Redis to SQLite for our caching layer.

Latency dropped 40%. Ops complexity dropped 90%.

Not every problem needs a distributed system.
What's the most "boring" tech decision that paid off for your team?
```

**Phoenix signals**: High dwell (counterintuitive claim makes people read carefully), strong reply magnet (question invites stories), bookmark-worthy (actionable insight), low negative risk.

---

## Announcement Post

**What makes it work**: Leads with user benefit, specific comparison, no link in body

```
Fine-tune a 7B model on your MacBook in 12 minutes.

v0.4 ships Metal 4 kernels + native ANE acceleration.
2.3× faster than MLX on M3 Max. Pure Rust. No Python. No cloud.

What model would you fine-tune first if you could do it locally?
```

**Phoenix signals**: Very high dwell (specific claims to process), reply magnet (genuine question), bookmark (people save tool recommendations), low negative (delivers on hook promise).

---

## Hot Take

**What makes it work**: Strong stance, brief reasoning, invites counter-arguments

```
Most "AI-powered" developer tools are just a ChatGPT wrapper with a $20/mo subscription.

The ones worth paying for do the boring stuff you'd never prompt for:
context management, codebase indexing, incremental re-analysis.

The wrapper tax is real. What tools are actually worth it?
```

**Phoenix signals**: High dwell (people read to see if they agree), extremely strong reply magnet (controversial + question), quote-tweet bait (people share their take), moderate negative risk (strong opinions polarize — acceptable tradeoff for engagement).

## Build-in-Public Update

**What makes it work**: Specific milestone, vulnerability, visual proof, forward-looking

```
Day 47: shipped the feature that almost made me quit.

Fused attention kernels for Metal. Tried 11 approaches.
The 12th worked — inference speed jumped 3.4×.

[screenshot of before/after benchmarks]

The gap between "this is impossible" and "this works" was one Sunday afternoon.
What's the feature that almost broke you?
```

**Phoenix signals**: Very high dwell (narrative + image), strong reply magnet (emotional question), bookmark-worthy (inspirational + technical), follow trigger (people want to see day 48).

---

## Quick Tip

**What makes it work**: Immediately actionable, specific time savings, humble tone

```
If you're running Docker on Mac and builds take forever:

```
DOCKER_BUILDKIT=1 docker build --platform linux/arm64 .
```

Just saved me 4 minutes per build. ARM-native layers skip emulation entirely.

I can't believe I didn't know this for 2 years.
```

**Phoenix signals**: High dwell (people try to memorize the command), extreme bookmark rate (save for later), moderate reply magnet (people share their own tips), near-zero negative risk.

## Thread Opener

**What makes it work**: Specific promise, credibility marker, clear structure

```
I've mass-analyzed 10,000 high-performing tech tweets.

7 patterns showed up in 80%+ of posts that broke 100K impressions.

None of them are "post consistently" or "be authentic."

Here's what actually matters ↓
```

**Phoenix signals**: Maximum dwell (people MUST read the thread), high bookmark (reference material), moderate reply magnet (people will quote specific findings), follow trigger (thread demonstrates expertise).

## Anti-Patterns — Posts That Look Good But Score Poorly

### The Vague Announcement
```
Excited to announce we just shipped a big update! 🚀
Lots of improvements and new features.
Check it out: [link]
Follow for more updates!
```
**Why it fails**: No specificity (low dwell), engagement bait ("follow for more"), link in body (30-50% penalty), no reply trigger, no reason to bookmark.

### The Over-Hashtagged
```
Just launched our new #AI #ML #MachineLearning tool for #developers!
#coding #tech #startup #opensource
Try it now!
```
**Why it fails**: Hashtag spam, no value proposition, no specificity, reads like an ad.

### The Thread That Should Be a Post
```
🧵 Thread on why testing is important (1/12)

1/ Testing is important because it catches bugs
2/ Bugs are bad for users
3/ Users who find bugs get frustrated
...
```
**Why it fails**: Obvious/shallow insights, padded length, numbered format looks formulaic, no unique perspective.
