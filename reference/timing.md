# Posting Timing — Data-Driven Optimization

## Source Data

Based on analysis of 700K-1M+ posts, updated for Q1 2026.

## Best Days to Post

| Rank | Day | Relative Engagement |
|------|-----|-------------------|
| 1 | Wednesday | Highest |
| 2 | Tuesday | Very high |
| 3 | Thursday | High |
| 4 | Monday | Above average |
| 5 | Sunday | Average |
| 6 | Friday | Below average |
| 7 | Saturday | Lowest |

**Key insight**: Midweek dominates. Weekends see lowest engagement across all niches.

## Best Times to Post (ET/US timezone)

### General Audience
| Time Window | Engagement Level | Best For |
|-------------|-----------------|----------|
| 8-10 AM ET | Peak | Professional/tech content, announcements |
| 12-1 PM ET | High | Lunch break scrolling — lighter content, polls |
| 5-6 PM ET | Moderate-High | End of workday — threads, longer content |
| 9 PM ET | Moderate | Evening leisure — stories, entertainment |

### B2B / Tech Audience
- **Primary**: Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11 AM ET
- **Secondary**: Wednesday, 12-1 PM ET
- **Avoid**: Weekends, after 8 PM

### Developer / OSS Audience
- **Primary**: Tuesday-Thursday, 10 AM - 12 PM ET
- **Secondary**: Weekday evenings 7-9 PM ET (side project time)
- **Bonus**: Sunday evening (people planning their week, exploring tools)

### General Consumer
- **Primary**: Wednesday 9 AM ET (single highest engagement slot)
- **Secondary**: Any weekday 8-10 AM ET
- **Tertiary**: Weekday evenings 8-10 PM ET

## Posting Frequency

### Optimal Volume
- **3-5 posts per week**: Quality-focused approach, best for small accounts
- **1-2 posts per day**: Growth mode, sustainable for active creators
- **3-5 posts per day**: Maximum without appearing spammy (mix: 1-2 original, 2-3 replies/quotes)

### Spacing Rules
- **Minimum 1-2 hours between posts** — diversity filter attenuates rapid-fire posts
- **Threads count as one post** — don't count individual tweets
- **Replies to others don't count** — reply farming is separate from posting cadence
- **More than 10 posts/day risks spam signals**

## The First-Hour Engagement Strategy

The first 30-90 minutes after posting are critical. Stack the odds:

1. **Post when your audience is active** — use the timing data above
2. **Be available to reply** — author replies are 75-150× multiplier
3. **Don't post and disappear** — the algo watches YOUR engagement in the first hour
4. **Stagger multi-post days** — post thread at 9 AM, single post at 1 PM
5. **Avoid posting right before logging off** — you need to be present for replies

## Timezone Considerations

If your audience is global:
- **US-focused**: Post 9-11 AM ET
- **EU-focused**: Post 3-5 PM CET (9-11 AM ET overlap)
- **Asia-Pacific**: Post 8-10 PM ET (morning APAC)
- **Global mix**: 9 AM ET captures US morning + EU afternoon

## Content-Type × Timing Matrix

| Content Type | Best Time | Reasoning |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|
| Threads | 8-9 AM ET weekdays | People have time to read during morning routine |
| Quick insights | 12-1 PM ET | Lunch break snackable content |
| Polls | 10-11 AM ET | Catches morning+afternoon voters |
| Announcements | 9-10 AM ET Tuesday-Thursday | Maximum professional visibility |
| Build-in-public | 5-7 PM ET | End of workday, people curious about others' progress |
| Hot takes | 12-2 PM ET | Lunch break engagement, people have time to argue |
| Video demos | 10 AM - 12 PM ET | Morning attention span is highest |

**Example**: A developer tools thread posted on Saturday at 11 PM ET got 600 impressions. The same quality thread reposted (with a new hook) on Wednesday at 9 AM ET got 8,400 impressions — 14× more reach purely from timing. The Wednesday post hit the critical window when the target audience (US developers) was actively scrolling during their morning routine.

## Analytics-Driven Adjustment

These are population-level optima. Individual accounts should adjust based on:

1. **Your audience's timezone** — check X Analytics for when followers are active
2. **Your niche** — developer audiences may peak later than marketing audiences
3. **Past performance** — track which posting times get the most first-hour engagement
4. **Consistency** — regular posting times train your audience to expect content
