# Reach Penalties — What Kills Distribution

## Severity Tiers

### Tier 1 — Immediate Reach Death
These will virtually guarantee your post underperforms:

**External Links in Main Post**
- 30-50% reach reduction for ANY external link
- Free accounts: zero median engagement on link posts since March 2025
- Premium accounts still penalized, just less severely
- **Fix**: Put links in first reply, or use "link in bio"
- **Example**: A bare GitHub link like `Check it out: https://github.com/user/repo` on a free account with 200 followers averaged 0 impressions in testing. The same post text without the link, with "Link in reply ↓" instead, reached 1,200+ impressions.

**Engagement Bait Phrases**
- "Follow for more" / "Like if you agree" / "RT to spread the word"
- Algorithm classifies these as low-quality signals
- Triggers reduced distribution even if initial engagement is decent
- **Fix**: Replace with genuine questions that invite discussion
- **Example**: "Like and RT if you agree!" → 12 likes, 0 reposts, 400 impressions. "What's your experience with this? Genuinely curious." → 8 likes, 14 replies, 3,200 impressions. Fewer likes but 8× the reach because replies are 13–27× more valuable.

**Spam Patterns via API**
- Posting more than ~10 posts/day
- Identical or near-identical content across multiple posts
- Automated likes/follows (heavily penalized in 2026)
- Posting at unnaturally regular intervals (bot signature)
- **Fix**: Post quality content at human-realistic intervals

### Tier 2 — Significant Reach Reduction
These meaningfully hurt distribution:

**Negative User Signals**
- Even 1-2 "Not Interested" clicks can outweigh dozens of likes
- Blocks and mutes from any user cascade to their network cluster
- Reports trigger content review and can suppress for hours/days
- **Fix**: Don't be controversial for the sake of controversy. Avoid topics that trigger visceral negative reactions from non-target audiences.

**Empty Media Posts**
- Images without context text (caption-only posts)
- Videos without any supporting text
- The algorithm needs text signals to classify and distribute
- **Fix**: Always pair media with substantive text

**Hashtag Abuse**
- 3+ hashtags looks spammy to the algorithm
- Irrelevant hashtags trigger "not interested" from mismatched audiences
- Hashtag-only posts (#AI #ML #Tech #Startup #Coding) = near-zero reach
- **Fix**: 0-2 relevant hashtags only
- **Example**: A post with `#AI #ML #LLM #Rust #Apple #OpenSource #Coding #DevTools` reached 80 impressions. The same post with zero hashtags reached 2,100. The hashtags attracted irrelevant audiences who clicked "Not Interested," tanking the score.

**Self-Reply Link Chains**
- Splitting content into self-reply chains with links in each
- The algorithm sees this pattern as promotional spam
- **Fix**: Use proper threads, consolidate links into one follow-up

### Tier 3 — Moderate Reach Impact
These subtly reduce performance:

**Poor Formatting**
- Walls of text with no line breaks
- All-caps posts
- Excessive emoji (3+ in non-casual contexts)
- **Fix**: Use whitespace, sentence fragments, visual rhythm

**Cross-Posting Artifacts**
- Obvious copy-paste from LinkedIn/Instagram (different tone, formatting)
- "Instagram-style" posts on X feel foreign and get lower engagement
- **Fix**: Write natively for X's culture and norms

**Posting at Low-Engagement Times**
- Weekend posts get ~40% less engagement than Wednesday peaks
- Late-night posts (11 PM - 6 AM audience timezone) miss the critical window
- **Fix**: Use timing data from reference/timing.md

**Frequency Overload**
- 5+ original posts per day without proportional engagement
- Multiple threads in one day
- **Fix**: Space posts, mix original content with replies/quotes

## The Negative Signal Cascade

Understanding how negative signals propagate:

```
User clicks "Not Interested" on your post
    ↓
Your post's score decreases for similar users
    ↓
Reduced distribution to that user's interest cluster
    ↓
Fewer impressions → fewer potential positive signals
    ↓
Post enters negative feedback loop
```

This is why **one post that gets negative signals can be worse than not posting at all.** The algorithm penalizes aggressively to protect user experience.

## Content That Triggers Negative Signals

Patterns that reliably produce "Not Interested" / Block / Mute:

1. **Off-topic for your niche** — If you're a tech creator, don't post political rants
2. **Repeated sales pitches** — Same product, same angle, multiple times per week
3. **Empty threads** — "Thread on X 🧵" followed by shallow/obvious takes
4. **Recycled viral content** — Reposting others' popular content as your own
5. **Excessive self-promotion** — Every post is about your product/service
6. **Misleading hooks** — Promise something in the hook, deliver something different
7. **Reply spam** — Mass-replying to popular accounts with self-promotional content

## Recovery from Negative Signal Accumulation

If your account has accumulated negative signals:

1. **Stop posting for 2-3 days** — let the negative momentum decay
2. **Resume with high-quality, non-promotional content** — genuine insights, helpful tips
3. **Focus on conversations** — reply thoughtfully to others in your niche
4. **Rebuild gradually** — 1 quality post per day for 2 weeks
5. **Monitor engagement rate, not impressions** — rate recovery indicates algorithm trust recovery

## The Safe Post Checklist

Before posting, verify:
- [ ] No external links in main post body
- [ ] No engagement bait phrases
- [ ] 0-2 relevant hashtags only
- [ ] Substantive text accompanies any media
- [ ] Content is on-topic for your audience
- [ ] Hook matches the actual content delivered
- [ ] Not the 4th+ post today without proportional engagement
- [ ] You'll be available to reply for the next 30-60 minutes
